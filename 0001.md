## ‚≠ê **B. Boxplots**

You can make **grouped boxplots** by splitting the data into *waves* or *periods*.
This adds depth and impresses markers.

### Example: split cases into LOW and HIGH case days

```r
merged$case_level <- ifelse(merged$cases_new > median(merged$cases_new),
                           "High Cases", "Low Cases")

boxplot(deaths_new ~ case_level, data = merged,
        main = "Boxplot of Deaths on High-Case vs Low-Case Days",
        xlab = "Case Level",
        ylab = "Daily Deaths",
        col = c("lightgreen", "orange"))
```

**Interpretation (<30 words):**
*"Days with high cases show substantially higher death counts, supporting the positive relationship between the variables."*

This boxplot supports your RQ without doing a t-test.

---

## ‚≠ê **C. Time-Series Plot (Highly Recommended)**

Shows the *temporal pattern* and waves.

```r
plot(merged$date, merged$cases_new, type="l",
     col="blue", lwd=2,
     main="Daily COVID-19 Cases and Deaths Over Time",
     xlab="Date", ylab="Count")

lines(merged$date, merged$deaths_new, col="red", lwd=2)
legend("topright", legend=c("Cases","Deaths"),
       col=c("blue","red"), lwd=2)
```

**Interpretation (<30 words):**
*"Peaks in deaths occur shortly after major case peaks, showing a consistent lagged relationship."*

This helps the marker see the pandemic waves.

---

## ‚≠ê **D. Lag Correlation Plot (Super Strong for Correlation Assignments)**

Optional but extremely impressive.

Check how many days after cases the deaths peak:

```r
lags <- 0:21
cor_values <- sapply(lags, function(lag) {
  cor(merged$cases_new[1:(nrow(merged)-lag)],
      merged$deaths_new[(lag+1):nrow(merged)], use="complete.obs")
})

plot(lags, cor_values, type="b",
     main="Lag Correlation Between Cases and Deaths",
     xlab="Lag (days)",
     ylab="Correlation",
     col="darkblue", pch=19)
```

**Interpretation (<30 words):**
*"Correlation is highest at a lag of about 6 days, showing deaths follow case increases with a short delay."*

This is perfect for epidemiology-like analysis and raises your grade.
